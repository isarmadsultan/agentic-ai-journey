LECTURE OVERVIEW
-----------------

Topic: Capitalism vs Liberalism
Duration: 60 minutes
Objectives:

* Understand the core principles of capitalism and liberalism
* Analyze the similarities and differences between the two ideologies
* Evaluate the impact of capitalism and liberalism on modern society

I. INTRODUCTION (5-10 minutes)
------------------------------

- Brief overview of capitalism and liberalism
- Importance of understanding these ideologies in today's world
- Preview of key concepts to be covered in the lecture

A. Definition and History
  - Capitalism: origin, development, and key figures
  - Liberalism: definition, history, and key figures

B. Key Principles
  - Capitalism: individual freedom, free market, limited government intervention
  - Liberalism: emphasis on equality, social justice, protection of individual rights

C. Lecture Objectives
  - Students will understand the core principles of capitalism and liberalism
  - Students will analyze the similarities and differences between the two ideologies
  - Students will evaluate the impact of capitalism and liberalism on modern society

II. CAPITALISM (15-20 minutes)
---------------------------

A. FREE MARKET ECONOMY
  - Definition: market forces determining prices and output
  - Characteristics: competition, supply and demand, entrepreneurship
  - Example: Adam Smith's "The Wealth of Nations"

B. ROLE OF GOVERNMENT IN A CAPITALIST SYSTEM
  - Limited government intervention: regulation, taxation, subsidies
  - Government's role in protecting property rights, enforcing contracts

C. ADVANTAGES OF CAPITALISM
  - Economic growth and innovation
  - Increased individual freedom and choice
  - Efficient allocation of resources

D. DISADVANTAGES OF CAPITALISM
  - Income inequality and poverty
  - Environmental degradation and exploitation
  - Risk of market failures and economic crises

III. LIBERALISM (15-20 minutes)
---------------------------

A. EMPHASIS ON INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS AND EQUALITY
  - Definition: protection of individual rights, social justice
  - Characteristics: emphasis on equality, human dignity
  - Example: John Rawls' "A Theory of Justice"

B. ROLE OF GOVERNMENT IN A LIBERAL SYSTEM
  - Government's role in protecting individual rights and freedoms
  - Regulation of markets to promote fairness and equality

C. ADVANTAGES OF LIBERALISM
  - Protection of minority rights and social justice
  - Promotion of human dignity and well-being
  - Encourages social welfare programs and public services

D. DISADVANTAGES OF LIBERALISM
  - Potential for excessive government intervention and regulation
  - May limit economic growth and innovation
  - Risk of moral relativism and cultural decay

IV. SIMILARITIES AND DIFFERENCES BETWEEN CAPITALISM AND LIBERALISM (10-15 minutes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. OVERLAPPING PRINCIPLES
  - Both capitalism and liberalism emphasize individual freedom and autonomy
  - Both ideologies recognize the importance of social justice and equality

B. KEY DIFFERENCES
  - Role of government: limited in capitalism, more active in liberalism
  - Economic focus: growth and innovation (capitalism), social welfare and equality (liberalism)

V. CONCLUSION AND DISCUSSION QUESTIONS (5-10 minutes)
---------------------------------------------------

A. Recap of key concepts
B. Discussion questions:
  - What are the advantages and disadvantages of capitalism and liberalism?
  - How do these ideologies impact modern society?
  - Can we balance individual freedom with social justice and equality?

SUGGESTED ACTIVITIES AND RESOURCES:

* Case studies: analyze real-world examples of capitalist and liberal systems
* Debate: discuss the role of government in a capitalist system
* Reading list:
